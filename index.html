About Jain University 

Jain (Deemed-to-be University), located in Bengaluru South, is a renowned educational institution known for its excellence in higher education. With a strong emphasis on holistic development and innovative teaching methods, Jain (Deemed-to-be University) offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across various disciplines. The university is known for its state-of-the-art infrastructure, experienced faculty, and industry-relevant curriculum. It provides students with ample opportunities for research, internships, and industry collaborations, ensuring they are well-prepared for their future careers. Jain (Deemed- to-be University) is committed to nurturing talent, fostering creativity, and shaping leaders of tomorrow.

International Conference on
Intelligent Computing Frontiers (ICICF - 2027) 

The International Conference on Intelligent Computing Frontiers (ICICF - 2027) aims to bring together researchers, practitioners, and industry experts to explore the evolving frontiers of intelligent computing. This multidisciplinary platform will cover emerging trends and technologies in artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, edge and cloud computing, IoT, cybersecurity, robotics, and intelligent systems. ICICF 2027 will foster collaboration across academia and industry, encouraging the exchange of innovative ideas, cutting-edge research, and real-world applications that drive digital transformation. Join us in January 2027 to shape the future of intelligent technologies and explore the limitless possibilities of computational intelligence.

Submission Guidelines 

1. Originality and Plagiarism

All submitted papers must be original, not previously published, and not under consideration for publication elsewhere. Submissions found to contain plagiarism or self-plagiarism will be rejected.

2. Registration and Presentation

Submission of a manuscript implies that at least one of the authors will register for the conference and present the paper if it is accepted. Papers not presented may be excluded from the final proceedings.

3. Full-length Papers and IEEE Compliance

Authors are encouraged to submit full-length papers to facilitate thorough peer review and ensure eligibility for publication in IEEE Xplore, subject to meeting IEEE quality and formatting standards.

Peer Review Process 

Paper Submission:

Authors must adhere to all formatting requirements and submission dates when submitting their research papers to the conference.

Initial Screening:

The conference organisers will verify the initial screening process to ensure that the submitted papers meet the fundamental requirements of the conference, such as relevance to the conference theme and adherence to the formatting guidelines.

Double-Blind Peer Review:

Each submitted paper is assigned to an expert double-blind reviewer in the subject by the programme chairs or conference organisers. Reviewers are chosen based on their experience and skill in the subject areas addressed by the conference.

Decision Making with Peer Review Process:

Reviewers carefully assess the papers based on factors such as experimental design, clarity of presentation, importance of the research, and technical soundness. They provide feedback and comments to authors to improve their contributions. Based on the feedback, the program chairs make a final decision, which may include acceptance, minor correction with acceptance, or rejection.

Standards:

Papers accepted to the conference will be involved in the proceedings and published with a collaborative publisher. Although publishable criteria may have slight differences among publishers, widely-accepted sets of criteria have been enforced in ICADIE 2024 for paper selection and publication to ensure high technical quality and ethical standards.

Language:

The manuscripts submitted to the conference should be written in English, which is required to be inclusive, appropriate, and understandable for editors, reviewers, and future readers. Please use proper grammar and fluent sentences, choose good words, and avoid syntax errors in your paper. Authors should be aware that comprehension difficulties may lead to the rejection of the paper.

Notification to Authors for Revision or Resubmission:

The results of the review process, including the acceptance or rejection decision and any required revisions, are sent to the authors within a specific timeframe. Authors are given the opportunity to make the necessary changes and submit their revised work to the conference.

Final Decision:

After authors submit their revised papers, the conference organisers evaluate them to ensure that the revisions adequately address the reviewers' suggestions. Authors are informed of the final acceptance decision, and approved papers are published either in the CCIS series or conference proceedings.
